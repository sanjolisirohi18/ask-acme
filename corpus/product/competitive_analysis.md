# Competitive Landscape Analysis

This document summarizes Acme Robotics' competitive positioning as of Q3 2024. It is maintained by Product Marketing and updated quarterly.

## Direct Competitors

### Symbotic

The most direct competitor in our segment. Symbotic targets enterprise retail (Walmart is their flagship customer) and has historically focused on greenfield builds rather than retrofits.

**Where we win:** Faster deployment time (12 weeks vs Symbotic's typical 8-14 months for greenfield), lower upfront cost, ability to work in existing facilities without major modifications.

**Where they win:** Throughput in very-high-volume facilities, brand recognition among Fortune 100 retailers, public company status (provides perceived stability).

### AutoStore

Goods-to-person robotic storage system. Different operating model from WX-7 (AutoStore uses fixed grid infrastructure) but addresses overlapping use cases.

**Where we win:** Flexibility (we don't require facility build-out), suitability for high-mix SKU environments, lower minimum facility size.

**Where they win:** Storage density, throughput for high-velocity SKUs in stable assortments.

### Locus Robotics

Closest competitor in terms of operating model. Locus uses collaborative mobile robots similar to our WX-7.

**Where we win:** Better integration ecosystem, more predictable pricing, stronger uptime track record.

**Where they win:** Larger installed base, longer relationships with major 3PLs, more mature European presence.

## Indirect Competitors

- **Manual labor** is our largest competitor in volume — most warehouses are still entirely manual.
- **Conveyor systems** remain dominant in legacy high-volume operations.
- **In-house solutions** from large customers (Amazon, Walmart, Target) are not addressable but shape the market.

## Win/Loss Trends

Q2 2024 win/loss data:

- Total opportunities: 47
- Wins: 23 (49%)
- Losses to Symbotic: 9 (19%)
- Losses to Locus: 5 (11%)
- Losses to manual / no decision: 10 (21%)

Common reasons for losses:

1. **Price** (32% of losses) — particularly for fleets under 25 robots
2. **Lack of cold storage capability** (24% of losses) — addressed in 2025 roadmap
3. **Reference customer concerns** in specific verticals — pharma and apparel notably underrepresented
