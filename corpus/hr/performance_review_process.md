# Performance Review Process

Acme runs performance reviews twice annually, in March and September. This document explains the process for both employees and managers.

## Timeline

- **Week 1-2:** Self-reviews submitted in Lattice
- **Week 3-4:** Peer feedback collection (3-5 peers, selected by employee in consultation with manager)
- **Week 5:** Managers draft initial ratings and write-ups
- **Week 6:** Calibration sessions across teams to ensure rating consistency
- **Week 7-8:** 1:1 review conversations between manager and direct report

## Rating Scale

Reviews use a five-point scale:

1. Below expectations
2. Approaching expectations
3. Meets expectations
4. Exceeds expectations
5. Significantly exceeds expectations

Approximately 60% of employees receive a 3 in any given cycle. Sustained ratings of 4 or 5 over multiple cycles are typically required for promotion consideration.

## Promotion Path

Promotions are evaluated separately from performance reviews, on a quarterly basis. To be considered for promotion, an employee must:

1. Be performing at the target level for at least one full review cycle (six months)
2. Be nominated by their manager
3. Pass a calibration review with peer managers
4. For senior promotions (E5+), present a "promotion packet" to a panel of E6+ engineers

## Compensation Adjustments

Compensation adjustments based on performance occur once annually, in April. Off-cycle adjustments require VP-level approval and are typically reserved for retention situations or scope changes.
