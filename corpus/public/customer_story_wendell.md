# Customer Story: How Wendell Foods Cut Picking Time by 38%

Wendell Foods is a fast-growing grocery distributor serving the Pacific Northwest. They operate a 400,000 square foot distribution center in Tacoma, Washington, fulfilling about 14,000 orders per day to grocery stores and restaurants.

Before deploying WX-7 robots in early 2024, Wendell's picking operation relied entirely on manual labor — about 75 pickers working two shifts, walking an average of 11 miles per shift. Picker turnover was running above 80% annually, which is typical for the industry but expensive: each new picker takes about three weeks to reach full productivity.

## The Deployment

Wendell started with a 40-robot pilot in their non-perishable goods area. The initial deployment took 14 weeks from contract to first production pick. During that time, Acme's deployment team worked on-site with Wendell to:

- Map the facility and identify navigation challenges
- Integrate with Wendell's existing Blue Yonder WMS
- Train operators on the Fleet Manager dashboard
- Adjust pick logic to account for Wendell's unique aisle configurations

## The Results

After six months of operation, Wendell measured the following changes:

- **Picking time per order:** down 38% on average
- **Pick accuracy:** up from 98.2% to 99.6%
- **Picker walking distance:** reduced by 71% (pickers now work in zones rather than full-facility)
- **Order fulfillment time:** reduced from 4.5 hours average to 2.8 hours
- **Headcount:** unchanged — pickers were reallocated to higher-value tasks rather than reduced

"We were skeptical at first," said Wendell's VP of Operations, Carlos Mendoza. "But the math worked out. We're shipping more, faster, with the same team. And our picker satisfaction scores actually went up because the work is less physically grueling."

## Looking Ahead

Wendell signed a contract in September 2024 to expand the deployment to 120 robots across all warehouse zones, including their refrigerated section once Acme's cold storage capability launches in 2025.
